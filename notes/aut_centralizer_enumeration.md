# Phase 3 enumeration: discrete centralizer and the SM factor-product structure

**Status:** STABLE (verified by
`src/utilities/automorphism_centralizer_extended.py`, 2026-05-15).
**Purpose:** Resolve Phase 3 of `notes/work_plan.md` (exact equality
vs containment in $\mathrm{Aut}_\text{ext}$).  The discrete-Hermitian
centralizer of the bipartite tick rule on $\mathbb{C}^{12}$ has
dimension 71 in $\mathfrak{su}(12)$ -- strictly larger than the
18-dim conjecture algebra of Eq.~(137).  Eq.~(137) holds as
$\supseteq$ at the discrete level; the equality $=$ requires
imposing the SM's factor-product reading (which the 59 extras
explicitly violate).
**Cited by:** the audit-table row "Exact equality vs containment
in $\mathrm{Aut}_\text{ext}$" in `paper/sections/audit_table.tex`
(updated from STUB to PART).

---

## What was computed

The per-site automorphism condition for an operator $X$ on
$\mathbb{C}^{12}$ to commute with the tick rule
$T_\text{ext} = is \cdot I_{12} + c \cdot (\sigma_x \otimes I_2
\otimes I_3)$ reduces (for all $\delta\phi$) to
$$[X,\; \sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3] = 0.$$

Using the tensor-product basis of $\mathfrak{su}(12)$ generated by
$\{\sigma_a, I_2\} \otimes \{\sigma_a, I_2\} \otimes \{\lambda_a, I_3\}$
(143 traceless generators), the script verifies that exactly
**71** basis elements commute with $\sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3$.
The result matches $\dim(\mathfrak{u}(6) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(6)) - 1 =
71$ from a direct eigenspace computation ($\sigma_x \otimes I_2
\otimes I_3$ has eigenvalues $\pm 1$ each with multiplicity 6, so
its centralizer is block-diagonal $\mathfrak{u}(6) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(6)$).

## Breakdown of the centralizer

The 71 generators split structurally as:

| Subset | Dim | Description |
|---|---|---|
| $J_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3$ | 1 | the only Hermitian rotation generator that commutes with $\sigma_x$ |
| $\mathfrak{su}(2)_W$: $I_2 \otimes \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_a \otimes I_3$ | 3 | acting on the isospin $\mathbb{C}^2$ |
| $\mathfrak{su}(3)_c$: $I_2 \otimes I_2 \otimes \tfrac{1}{2}\lambda_a$ | 8 | acting on the colour $\mathbb{C}^3$ |
| isospin-colour mixings: $I_2 \otimes \sigma_a \otimes \lambda_b$ | 24 | $a \in \{1,2,3\}$, $b \in \{1,\ldots,8\}$ |
| chirality-$\sigma_x$ + isospin: $\sigma_x \otimes \sigma_a \otimes I_3$ | 3 | |
| chirality-$\sigma_x$ + colour: $\sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes \lambda_a$ | 8 | |
| chirality-$\sigma_x$ + iso-col: $\sigma_x \otimes \sigma_a \otimes \lambda_b$ | 24 | |
| **Total** | **71** | |

The first 12 (1 + 3 + 8) are the conjecture's Hermitian SU(3) ⊕ SU(2) ⊕ ⟨J_1⟩ generators.  The remaining 59 are factor-mixing operators not in the conjecture's algebra.

## The 71-dim algebra is structurally $\mathfrak{su}(6) \oplus \mathfrak{su}(6) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$

A sharper structural identification (added 2026-05-15 in response to
the structural reading by an external reviewer):

In the $\sigma_x$-eigenbasis decomposition $\mathbb{C}^{12} =
\mathbb{C}^6_+ \oplus \mathbb{C}^6_-$ (where $\mathbb{C}^6_\pm$
are the $\pm 1$ eigenspaces of $\sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3$),
the centralizer is block-diagonal with $\mathfrak{u}(6)_\pm$ acting
on each block.  Decomposing $\mathfrak{u}(6) = \mathfrak{su}(6)
\oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$ and identifying the overall trace as
outside $\mathfrak{su}(12)$:
$$\boxed{\;\mathfrak{Aut}_{\text{centralizer}} \;=\; \mathfrak{su}(6)_+ \oplus \mathfrak{su}(6)_- \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1),\qquad \dim = 35 + 35 + 1 = 71.\;}$$
The two $\mathfrak{su}(6)$ factors act on the $\pm 1$ chirality
eigenspaces (each $\mathbb{C}^6 = \mathbb{C}^2_{\text{iso}} \otimes
\mathbb{C}^3_{\text{col}}$), and the $\mathfrak{u}(1)$ is the
difference-of-traces between the two blocks, generated by
$J_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3$ -- i.e.\
$J_1$ is the central U(1) of the centralizer.

The bipartite parity $\sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3$ itself acts
as the **left-right exchange** of the two $\mathfrak{su}(6)$
factors, in close analogy to (but not identical with) left-right
symmetric grand unification models like Pati-Salam
$SU(4)_C \times SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$.  The framework recovers
this LR-symmetric SU(6)-pair structure from the $\mathcal{A}=1$
constraint on the bipartite octahedral lattice, not from
postulated gauge content.

## SU(3) × SU(2) decomposition of each $\mathfrak{su}(6)$ factor

The SM gauge group $SU(3)_c \times SU(2)_W \times U(1)_Y$ has a
natural embedding in $SU(6)$ via the tensor decomposition
$\mathbf{6} = (\mathbf{3}, \mathbf{2})$.  Under $SU(3) \times SU(2)$,
the adjoint representation $\mathbf{35}$ of $SU(6)$ branches as
$$\mathbf{35} \;=\; (\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{1}) \oplus (\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{3}) \oplus (\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{3}),$$
with dimensions $8 + 3 + 24 = 35$.  Identifying each piece in the
framework's basis:

- $(\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{1})$: the 8 SU(3)\_c colour generators
  $\lambda_a$ acting on $\mathbb{C}^3_{\text{col}}$ with isospin
  identity.  These are SM gauge.
- $(\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{3})$: the 3 SU(2)\_W isospin generators
  $\sigma_a$ acting on $\mathbb{C}^2_{\text{iso}}$ with colour
  identity.  These are SM gauge.
- $(\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{3})$: the 24 mixed generators $\sigma_a
  \otimes \lambda_b$ acting on $\mathbb{C}^2_{\text{iso}} \otimes
  \mathbb{C}^3_{\text{col}}$ non-trivially in both factors.  These
  are the "leptoquark-flavoured" factor-mixing operators -- they
  transform as $(\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{3})$ of the SM gauge group,
  which is the colour-octet weak-triplet, exactly the
  representation that GUT-style leptoquark generators carry.

The $(\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{3})$ identification matches one half of
the centralizer's extras: 24 of the 59 are leptoquark-irrep
generators in the internal block, and the same decomposition holds
in the $\sigma_x$-block ($\sigma_x \otimes \sigma_a \otimes \lambda_b$
also transforms as $(\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{3})$).  Total leptoquark
extras: $24 + 24 = 48$.

The remaining $59 - 48 = 11$ extras are:
- 8 $\sigma_x \otimes I_2 \otimes \lambda_a$: SM-colour-flavoured
  generators in the $\sigma_x$ block (chirality-flip versions of
  the SU(3)\_c colour generators).
- 3 $\sigma_x \otimes \sigma_a \otimes I_3$: SM-isospin-flavoured
  generators in the $\sigma_x$ block (chirality-flip versions of
  SU(2)\_W).

These 11 are the $\sigma_x$-twisted SM-flavoured generators -- they
have the same SU(3)×SU(2) representation content as the SM gauge
generators ($(\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{1})$ and $(\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{3})$),
but they are *not* SM gauge because they couple to the chirality
$\sigma_x$ block instead of the chirality $I$ block.

## What's missing from the centralizer

Of Eq.~(137)'s 18 generators, 4 are *not* in the discrete centralizer:

- $J_2 = \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_y \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3$ -- chirality is $\sigma_y$, anticommutes with $\sigma_x$
- $J_3 = \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_z \otimes I_2 \otimes I_3$ -- chirality is $\sigma_z$, anticommutes with $\sigma_x$
- $K_1, K_2, K_3$ -- anti-Hermitian Lorentz boosts; excluded from $\mathfrak{u}(12)$

The Lorentz rotations $J_2, J_3$ are *continuum-emergent* in Paper~I's
framework (via $O_h$-averaging of the discrete spatial automorphism
group).  They are not symmetries of the discrete bipartite tick
rule.  The boosts $K_a$ are anti-Hermitian on the chirality
$\mathbb{C}^2$ and live in $\mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{C})$ rather
than $\mathfrak{u}(2)$ -- they generate hyperbolic, not unitary,
chirality transformations.

The Hermitian U(1) overall phase ($I_{12}$) is the trace, outside
$\mathfrak{su}(12)$; in $\mathfrak{u}(12)$ it adds 1 to the
centralizer (giving dim 72) and 1 to the conjecture's relevant
generators (giving 13 in centralizer).

## The 59 extras as factor-mixing operators

The 59 generators in the centralizer that are NOT in the conjecture
all share one feature: they couple tensor factors that the
conjecture treats as independent.

- The 24 *isospin-colour* mixings $I_2 \otimes \sigma_a \otimes \lambda_b$
  couple SU(2)\_W flavor structure to SU(3)\_c colour structure.
  In the SM, such operators would violate $SU(2)_W \times SU(3)_c$
  gauge invariance -- they correspond to flavor-colour-mixing
  interaction terms which the SM Lagrangian explicitly excludes.
- The 35 (= 3 + 8 + 24) *chirality-$\sigma_x$* extras couple the
  chirality factor (in the parity-eigenstate basis) to isospin
  and/or colour.  These would be analogous to operators that
  treat $\psi_R - \psi_L$ as having internal-gauge content,
  which the SM also forbids by its gauge structure.

So the 59 extras are precisely the operators that the SM's
factor-product gauge structure forbids by construction.  Equivalently:
**Eq.~(137)'s equality $=$ is recovered iff the SM's factor-product
gauge invariance is imposed as an additional constraint beyond
tick-rule commutativity**.  The lattice's discrete tick rule alone
does not single out the SM's gauge-product structure.

## Phase 3 outcome class

**PART.**  The conjecture's $\supseteq$ from prior PASS rows is
confirmed and sharpened (the discrete centralizer is now known
explicitly to be dim 71, not just $\geq 18$).  Equality $=$ is
*conditional* on imposing the SM factor-product gauge-invariance
constraint.  Audit-table row updated STUB $\to$ PART.

The structural reading: the lattice's $\mathcal{A}=1$ tick rule
admits a larger algebra of symmetries than the SM gauge group.
The SM gauge structure is the *factor-irreducible* sub-symmetry
that does not mix the conjecture's tensor factors.  Whether this
factor-product structure is naturally imposed by the lattice (e.g.,
by continuum-emergent Lorentz combined with basis-permutation
symmetry) or is an external interpretive choice is a structural
question the framework hands to follow-up work.

## Connection to prior Phase results

This finding is consistent with the Phase 1 + Phase 1.5 results:

- Phase 1 Route (a) found bipartite parity = spatial parity (not
  chirality projection); $SU(2)_W$ couples vector-like.  Both are
  consistent with the lattice admitting a larger automorphism
  algebra than the SM expects.
- Phase 1.5 ruled out the antilinear $CP$ modification because no
  $C$ anticommutes with all Gell-Manns.  This is structurally
  separate from Phase 3's finding: Phase 1.5 is about whether the
  lattice can incorporate a *specific* discrete symmetry ($CP$);
  Phase 3 is about whether the lattice's centralizer is
  *unnecessarily large* compared to Eq.~(137).
- Phase 2 showed the natural colour-memory tick rule generates the
  full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ as a dynamically-active subalgebra (not
  just as a global symmetry).  Phase 3 confirms that the full
  $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ sits inside the centralizer (it must, since
  $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ commutes with the chirality factor).

## Connection to mass-chirality coupling

The mass-chirality coupling note (`notes/mass_chirality_coupling.md`)
already established that the bipartite tick rule's chirality-mixing
is *the* mass mechanism, and that vector-like gauge coupling is
forced by it.  Phase 3 sharpens this: the centralizer's 59
factor-mixing extras are operators that *would* violate gauge
invariance if treated as physical interactions, but the lattice
admits them at the kinematic level.  The factor-product SM gauge
structure is the cleanest way to forbid these extras and recover
Eq.~(137) as equality.

This suggests the framework's terminal prediction is:

> *The lattice's discrete tick rule admits a 71-dim algebra of
> per-site automorphisms.  The 18-dim SM gauge + Lorentz subalgebra
> is the factor-product-irreducible sub-symmetry.  The remaining
> 53 (= 71 - 18 + 4 missing-from-discrete) generators correspond

> for follow-up work.*

  35 (chirality-identity) + 36 (chirality-$\sigma_x$).  The

  respects the SM gauge structure naturally restricts to the
  conjecture's 18 generators; one that allows factor-mixings could
  access the full 71-dim algebra, but at the cost of SM gauge
  invariance.

## Open follow-up

The natural continuum-emergent constraints that might reduce the
71-dim centralizer to the conjecture's 18 are:

1. $S_3$ basis-permutation symmetry on the colour basis vectors,
   which restricts the colour factor to $S_3$-invariant
   operators.  Quick estimate: cuts dim of colour algebra from 9
   ($\mathfrak{u}(3)$) to ~2 ($S_3$-invariants), removing many of
   the 24 + 24 colour-involving mixings.
2. Lorentz emergence via $O_h$-averaging, which restores $J_2,
   J_3$ (and possibly $K_2, K_3$) to the algebra.
3. Discrete spatial $O_h$ symmetry constraints on chirality-
   involving operators.

A future script could impose these constraints in stages and
verify whether the dim reduces to 18.  This is not done in the
present Phase 3 script; the present script's contribution is to
state explicitly what the discrete-level centralizer is, leaving
the continuum-emergent reduction for later work.
