# Non-abelian SU(3) generation from the per-site colour memory

**Status:** STABLE (verified by
`src/utilities/su3_generation_from_colour_memory.py`, 2026-05-15).
**Purpose:** Resolve Phase 2 of `notes/work_plan.md` (non-abelian
$SU(3)$ generation from the $\mathbb{C}^3$ memory).  The lattice's
natural colour-memory tick rule does generate the full
$\mathfrak{su}(3)$ when the rule has real-symmetric off-diagonal
content; strictly restricted choices (diagonal-only,
imaginary-antisymmetric-only) yield strict subalgebras.
**Cited by:** the audit-table row "Non-abelian SU(3) from
$\mathbb{C}^3$ memory" in `paper/sections/audit_table.tex` (PASS).
Will be folded into Paper II's prose as part of the established-
factors discussion once Phase 3 closes.

## Setting

The proposed colour-memory mechanism: when the wavefunction
traverses spatial direction $\mathbf{V}_j$, the tick rule applies a
unitary $U_j$ on the per-site colour $\mathbb{C}^3$, with the
$\{U_1, U_2, U_3\}$ related by the lattice's $S_3$ basis symmetry
($U_j = P_{1 \to j}\, U_1\, P_{1 \to j}^{-1}$).

The question Phase 2 settles: what Lie subalgebra of
$\mathfrak{su}(3)$ is generated by the infinitesimal generators
$X_j = -i \log U_j$ under Lie-bracket closure?

The answer determines whether the lattice DYNAMICALLY GENERATES
the full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ of Eq.~(137) (as required for the
SM gauge group factor to be a derived feature, not just a global
symmetry of the trivial extension verified in
`automorphism_direct_product_extended.py`).

## What the script verifies

Seven candidate $X_1$'s tested via sympy.  Closure dimensions:

| Candidate $X_1$ | Orbit dim | Closure dim | Subalgebra |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0$ (trivial) | 0 | 0 | trivial |
| $\lambda_8$ (Cartan) | 2 | 2 | Cartan $\mathfrak{h}$ |
| $P_1 - I/3$ (diagonal, $|1\rangle$-singling) | 2 | 2 | Cartan $\mathfrak{h}$ |
| $\lambda_2$ (single imag off-diag) | 2 | 3 | $\mathfrak{su}(2)_{\text{imag}}$ |
| $\lambda_1$ (single real off-diag) | 2 | 3 | one $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ |
| $\lambda_2 + \lambda_5$ (imag, rotate-toward-$|1\rangle$) | 2 | 3 | $\mathfrak{su}(2)_{\text{imag}}$ |
| $\lambda_1 + \lambda_4$ (real, rotate-toward-$|1\rangle$) | 3 | 8 | full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ |
| $\lambda_8 + \lambda_1$ (Cartan + real off-diag) | 2 | 8 | full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ |

Three structural cases emerge:

- **Diagonal-only:** generates the abelian Cartan $\mathfrak{h}$
  (dim 2), the continuous closure of the discrete RGB $\mathbb{Z}_3$
  finding of `automorphism_rgb_su3.py`.
- **Off-diagonal in a single Gell-Mann direction:** generates one
  $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ subalgebra (dim 3) -- the closure of the
  $S_3$-orbit of a single off-diagonal involves all three
  rotational planes' generators and closes to one $\mathfrak{su}(2)$,
  not three independent ones.
- **Real-symmetric two-plane (rotate-toward-$|j\rangle$ coherently),
  or any mix containing real-symmetric off-diagonal content:**
  generates the **full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$** (dim 8) under
  Lie-bracket closure.

## Why the natural reading lands at full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$

The physical content of "colour memory updates when the
wavefunction traverses $\mathbf{V}_j$" is: amplitude is *rotated
toward* component $|j\rangle$ from the other components.  The
minimal unitary that realises this is a real-symmetric rotation in
the two off-diagonal planes that involve index $j$: for $j = 1$,
the generator is $\lambda_1 + \lambda_4$ (mixing $|1\rangle$ with
$|2\rangle$ in the first plane and with $|3\rangle$ in the second).
The imaginary-antisymmetric reading $\lambda_2 + \lambda_5$ is a
different rotation in the same planes, and physically corresponds
to introducing a $\pi/2$ phase shift during the rotation -- less
natural for a coherent-memory update.

The script verifies that the real-symmetric reading's Lie closure
captures the full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$: starting from
$\{\lambda_1 + \lambda_4, \lambda_6 + \lambda_1, \lambda_4 + \lambda_6\}$
(the $S_3$ orbit of $\lambda_1 + \lambda_4$ under cyclic
permutations of the colour basis), the brackets pull in the
imaginary off-diagonals $\lambda_{2,5,7}$ (via
$[\lambda_1, \lambda_4] = i\lambda_7$, $[\lambda_1, \lambda_6] =
i\lambda_5$, $[\lambda_4, \lambda_6] = i\lambda_2$), then the
Cartan elements $\lambda_3, \lambda_8$ (via further brackets like
$[\lambda_1, \lambda_2] = 2i\lambda_3$ and $[\lambda_4, \lambda_5]
= i(\lambda_3 + \sqrt{3}\lambda_8)$).

## Robustness: even minimal mixed candidates close to $\mathfrak{su}(3)$

The candidate $X_1 = \lambda_8 + \lambda_1$ (one Cartan element
plus one real off-diagonal, the latter in the (1,2) plane) has an
$S_3$ orbit of only dimension 2 (because $\lambda_8$ is $S_3$-
invariant and $\lambda_1$ is invariant under the transposition
$(1\, 2)$).  Yet its Lie closure is dim 8.  This is the key
robustness result: *any* colour-memory tick rule with non-trivial
real-symmetric content closes to full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ under
Lie brackets, regardless of how small the $S_3$ orbit is at the
generator level.

The only ways to *avoid* full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ are highly
restricted: either purely diagonal (Cartan only), or purely
imaginary antisymmetric (which closes to $\mathfrak{su}(2)$
because the imaginary-antisymmetric Gell-Manns
$\{\lambda_2, \lambda_5, \lambda_7\}$ form a closed
$\mathfrak{so}(3) \cong \mathfrak{su}(2)$ subalgebra of
$\mathfrak{su}(3)$, with commutators $[\lambda_2, \lambda_5] =
i\lambda_7$ etc.).  These are structurally distinct from the
"natural" colour-memory readings.

## Relationship to Eq.~(137) and the framework's contribution

The Eq.~(137) statement requires $SU(3)$ as an automorphism factor.
Phase 2 establishes that this $SU(3)$ is GENERATED by the natural
lattice dynamics (not merely admitted as a global symmetry of a
trivial colour extension).  The framework's contribution:

1. The full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ is structurally available in the
   colour memory $\mathbb{C}^3$, for any natural real-symmetric
   colour-memory tick rule.  This is robust: a 2-dim seed can
   close to dim 8 under Lie brackets.
2. The framework does NOT uniquely select which specific
   colour-memory mechanism applies.  Multiple candidates with
   different physical interpretations all yield the same closed
   algebra.  Phase 2 reads this as a robustness feature
   ($\mathfrak{su}(3)$ doesn't depend on tick-rule fine-tuning),
   not a deficiency.
3. The structural alternatives --- Cartan only or one
   $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ subalgebra --- are sharply distinguished
   from the full-$\mathfrak{su}(3)$ regime, and correspond to
   physically degenerate tick rules (no off-diagonal mixing, or
   only $\pi/2$-shifted rotations).  The framework predicts that
   any rotation-coherent colour-memory rule lands in the full
   $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ regime.

## Upstream-flow tags

- **Algebra:** the closure of an $S_3$-orbit of a Hermitian
  traceless $3\times 3$ matrix under Lie brackets depends on the
  representation-theoretic content of the seed (Cartan / real
  off-diagonal / imaginary off-diagonal).  Real-symmetric seeds
  close generically; imaginary-antisymmetric seeds close to a
  $\mathfrak{so}(3) \cong \mathfrak{su}(2)$ subalgebra.  This is
  a generic feature of $\mathfrak{su}(N)$ under permutation-
  subgroup orbits and may have analogues for $\mathfrak{su}(N)$
  at higher $N$ in future generalisations.
- **Topology / structure of the bipartition:** the colour-memory
  tick rule's coherent rotation reading is the natural extension
  of the bipartite tick's chirality mixing to a three-fold
  branching --- the colour-mixing analogue of the chirality $\sigma_x$
  amplitude that becomes mass via clock density.  Worth recording
  whether the colour-memory tick has its own mass-analogue
  amplitude in a future paper.
- **Balanced equations:** an $\mathcal{A}=1$ reaction that involves
  colour-changing processes (e.g., quark colour exchange) inherits
  the full $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ closure as the algebra of admissible
  amplitude rotations; the discrete RGB symmetry of
  `automorphism_rgb_su3.py` is the abelian Cartan part of this.
